Graphs can reveal where functions cross the x-axis, indicating roots. When two functions, such as y = x³ and y = 4 − x, intersect, the x-coordinate of the intersection satisfies both equations, so it’s a root of x³ + x − 4 = 0.

To verify a root exists between a and b, check that f(a) and f(b) have opposite signs. This guarantees at least one root in the interval.

The change of sign method depends on the fact that if f(a) and f(b) have opposite signs, then a root must exist between them.

The bisection method repeatedly halves the interval [a,b], choosing the subinterval where a sign change occurs, until the interval is sufficiently small.
